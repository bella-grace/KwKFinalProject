Our Mission

HomeMore works to educate the public on the severity of homelessness whether chronic or temporary. It is a topic not many are aware of and our goal is to change that through providing accurate resources and statistics.

Why is Homelessness an Issue You Should Care About?

Simple.
It is a basic human right to have access to safe and affordable housing.
Although 2% of the global population might seem like a small amount, to put that in perspective, that's 140 million people!
Homelessness is an issue that a numerous amount of people worldwide face everyday. And with it come other struggles like food insecurity, and exposure to the elements. Not having an address makes it difficult for homeless people to get a job as many job applications require that the applicant supply a "permanent address." And without a source of income it difficult to afford a place to live.
Currently with this pandemic the homeless is one the most vulnerable populations to COVID-19, as they don't have anywhere clean and safe to sleep/quarantine, or easy access to running water so that they can wash their hands.

Unfortunately, homelessness is a difficult issue to solve for a variety of reasons:
1) There is no single agreed upon definition of the word, which makes it difficult to determine who is homeless (who needs support/help). Also countries have different cultures, and "standards" of living this means that there is no "one" solution to remedying homelessness.
2) Each country has different circumstances which cause homelessness from a lack of affordable housing to social and environmental crises.
3) Providing affordable housing for all would probably require the support of the government, and many are reluctant to address/solve the issue.

North America

According to the Homeless Hub, an estimated 550,000 individuals in the United States and 235,000 in Canada experience homelessness within a year and have inadequate housing on any given night, with the leading cause being a lack of affordable housing. Between 13,000 and 33,000 are chronically homeless.
The two leading causes of homelessness in North America is lack of affordable housing, and unemployemnt.

South America

It is extremely difficult to collect accurate data revolving around the homeless population in South America. However, poverty and homelessness are closely linked and in South America, an estimated 23.8% of people live under the poverty line. With individual countries ranging from 3 - 74% .
Women, especially women who live alone are the most susceptible population to become homeless and/or live in poverty.

Europe

According to FEANTSA, at least 700,000 homeless people are sleeping rough or in emergency/temporary accommodation on one night in the European Union.
It is an increase of 70% compared to ten years ago.
Over the past two decades, several initiatives at the European Union level have helped to include homelessness as an important topic on the EU agenda.
Principle 19 on “Housing and assistance for the homeless” in the European Pillar of Social Rights (EPSR) recognises the right of the homeless to housing and assistance.

Africa

In Africa there are an estimated number of 34,200,000+ homeless people living on the street alone, and 422,000,000 people living below the global poverty line.
These are just estimations as multiple countries in Africa haven’t stated or updated their statistics on the homeless population. Africa is a huge continent that is very diverse, it contains 54 countries (UN), and is home to over 1.2 billion people. Each country in Africa is unique, and each has it's own assets, and issues.

Oceania/Australia

Australia’s definition of homeless inlcudes not only not having a roof over your head but also inadequate shelter, unavailability of housing longterm, and lack of control over access.
There are about 116,000 people in Australia who are homeless. The greatest cause to homelessness in Australia is domestic violence. Visit this site to learn more!

Asia

There doesn't appear to be reliable data on the number of homeless people in Asia, but the #1 city in the world with the greatest homeless population is Manila, which is located in the Phillipines. There are an estimated 3.1 million homeless there. Asia like Africa is also a very big continent, it contains 48 countries (UN), and is home to over 4.4 billion people.
Several Asian countries have implemented changes and plans in an attempt to reduce their homeless population. Their plans include relocating over 30 million people in China alone from rural to urban areas to try and eradicate extreme poverty. Many have also created incentives to convince people to visit centers for help.

Charity Project: Help Bristol’s Homeless (UK)

Their mission is to get homeless people off the street first, and then give them a helping hand in the right direction.
This charity has created a gated community of self-contained micro flats from shipping containers, where they are able to offer the amenities needed to support the residents.
Jasper Thompson, the founder, works with the support of the directors, trustees, staff and volunteers to build temporary accommodation, to be used by residents while they access the help they need to secure a permanent home. By providing users with the security and dignity that everyone deserves, they empower them to take an active role in improving their situation.

Resources + More Organizations
  • The Institute of Global Homelessness is an organization dedicated to a global movement to end street homelessness, and create a framework to define homelessness.

  • The National Alliance to End Homelessness is a great website. It has endless amounts of resources and statistics on homelessness in the US.

  • The Homeless Hub is a Canadian organization with information ranging from statistics, to volunteering, to possible solutions.

  • Food Not Bombs is a global volunteer ran organization, dedicated to feeding the hungry worldwide. Check out their website to find locations where you can volunteer!

  • United Way is an US based organization that works for the common good, and creates opportunities for all.

  • Covenant House is an organization that works to help the homeless youth.

  • True Colors United is an organization bringing awareness to the fact that LGBTQ youth are at higher risk to become homeless. They go into communities and educate, as well as implement systems to prevent youth homelessness.

  • Network for Good is an organization that shares information on other organizations in the local African community to help end poverty and disease in Africa.

  • Goodwill is a second-hand store that has locations all over the US. And it is a great place to donate used professional clothing that is in good condition, so that lower income people can afford nice business wear for their job or job interview.

  • Hope - Homeless People is a Nongovernmental Organization consisting of homeless people and working against homelessness in Europe. It is a network for all people in Europe, who are, or have been homeless, including people in (immediate) danger of becoming homeless.

  • Mission Australia is an integrated nationwide service to help people find safe and affordable housing, support disadvantaged children and families, empower troubled young people, assist people with mental illness and disability, and much more. Early intervention and prevention is at the heart of our work.

  • Nanoom House is an Korean organization which aims to help Korean immigrants who have become homeless due to any circumstances. They stand behing their belief that everyone must have a place to sleep, have access to food, and be given the opportunity to recover.
